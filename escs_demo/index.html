Abstract

Developing AI systems to aid children with SLP (Speech-Language Pathology) issues is a promising area of research. The Early Social Communication Scales (ESCS) assessment holds significant importance in the early detection of communication and social interaction issues in children. In this demo, we design a multimodal video understanding system for the ESCS assessment context. To that end, we recorded and annotated a total of 8 videos imitating the examiner-child interaction for 4 standard tasks under ESCS: hat, wind-up toy, picture-on-the-wall, and book. Moreover, we carefully designed system instructions for an LLM-based model and equipped it with various tools to effectively understand the videos in the context of ESCS. We believe our system is a promising step in developing an AI Screener.

ESCS Behaviors

We provide the definitions for the behaviors that we aim to detect in the recorded videos.

Initiating Behavioral Request (IBR): the child's skill in using behavior(s) to elicit aid in obtaining an object.

  • behaviors coded: eye-contact; point; language; reach; give

Initiating Joint Attention (IJA): the child's skill in using behavior(s) to initiate shared attention to objects or events.

  • behaviors coded: alternating gaze; coordinated joint look; language; point; show; give

Responding to Joint Attention (RJA): the child's skill in following the examiner's line of regard and pointing gestures.

  • behaviors coded: alternating gaze; language

Initiating Social Interaction (ISI): the child's skill at initiating turn-taking sequences and the tendency to tease the tester.

  • behaviors coded: turn-taking

Responding to Social Interaction (RSI): the child's skill in responding to turn-taking interactions initiated by the examiner.

  • behaviors coded: responds to invitation

System Diagram

We equip our system with various tools for understanding and detecting children behaviours in the ESCS assessment context. Specifically, we use WhisperX for Automated Speech Recognition (ASR) to obtain video tramnscripts to pass as extra context to the LLM-based Video-LLaVA model for improved performance. We also include ESCS behavior definitions along with the objects used in the video in our system prompt.
